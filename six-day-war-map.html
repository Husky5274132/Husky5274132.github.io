The Six-Day War began on June 5, 1967, with Israel launching a preemptive military strike against Egypt and Syria. This map shows the territorial control and military positions at the start of the conflict.

Key Facts at War Onset:

• Israeli Preemptive Strike: Israel launched Operation Focus, destroying much of Arab air forces on the ground
• Armed Forces Imbalance: Arabs outnumbered Israelis but lacked coordination
• Territorial Stakes: Control of Sinai, Gaza, Golan Heights, and West Bank
• Duration: Despite numerical disadvantage, Israel achieved victory in just 6 days
• Outcome: Israeli territorial expansion; Arab states' military humiliation
